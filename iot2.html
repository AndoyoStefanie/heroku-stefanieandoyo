Internet of Things

What are some trends in the Internet of Things?




1. Logitech Harmony Universal Remote

Harmony is a powerful and useful IoT smart device for daily purposes. It is a universal remote which lets you control your house media, lighting, and other smart devices from one location remotely. It has features of up to 8 remotes, reduces complexity and clusters in-house. It supports more than 5000 brands and new brands can also be added in the future.



2. Kuri Mobile Robot

Kuri is a home robot designed to interact with you and your family and capture clips of your day. It has an expressive personality and its own unique robot language. It was created by Mayfield Robotics in the USA. Capacitive touch sensors let Kuri respond to human touch in a truly personal way. A gentle tap to the head will make Kurt look up at you and chirp affirmingly.





3. Philips Hue Bulbs and Lighting System

Philips Hue is a line of smart LED light bulbs and fixtures. Each one communicates wirelessly with the
Hue Bridge, a little modem-looking thing that you keep plugged into your home's router. That connection
to the cloud lets you control Hue's lights from your phone, with a voice command via Siri, Alexa or the
Google Assistant, or by automating them to turn on and off at specific times or when other devices
trigger them. Many of Philips Hue's bulbs and fixtures can change colors upon request (hence the "Hue"
branding), but some are just basic bulbs that put out plain ol' white light and nothing else.
